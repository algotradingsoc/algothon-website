FAQs

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a 24-hour competition where programmers and researchers come together to build and design something cool.

What is Algothon?

It is the largest student-led financial machine learning hackathon in Europe.
The competition is 24 hours of innovation to design the best alpha-gen models using the latest machine learning and quant techniques inside BlackRock's London office.

Who is eligible?

Any students, academics and recent graduates over 18 are welcome to apply.

Do I need to be studying at Imperial College London?

No, students, academics and recent grads are welcome from any university.

Do I need a background in finance?

No. Whilst financial knowledge can be beneficial, it is not necessary.
You will learn most of the core knowledge from event through researcher presentations, workshops, and the Algothon team.

Do I need to be able to code?

Yes. Having a knowledge of basic coding principles is important to implement your strategies and research. However, if you need help on the day feel free to reach out through the support system!

Do I need to have a team beforehand?

No, but you are welcome to form one. Last year, many teams were formed in the event after meeting and sharing ideas and goals.

What is the maximum team size?

Teams of up to 4 are allowed.

Where is the event?

Due to the ongoing COVID situation, Algothon this year will be run remotely.

Can I volunteer at the event?

Yes, volunteers are very welcome. Just email algo.trade@imperial.ac.uk if you are interested.

When will applications close?

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis and will close on TBD

Any other questions?

Please email algo.trade@imperial.ac.uk.
